About Me

Hi, I am Changzhi Jiang, currently pursuing my undergraduate degree in Physics at the Institute for Advanced Study, Shenzhen University, under the supervision of Researcher Chunyu Ding. My research focuses on radar detection, water ice, and habitability in the landing area of Mars’ Tianwen-1 mission. I have authored two papers (one in Space Science Reviews and another under review in Nature Astronomy) as the first author, and contributed to four additional papers published or under review in prestigious journals such as Remote Sensing, The Astronomical Journal, and Icarus. I am currently leading or participating in two national-level Undergraduate Innovation and Entrepreneurship Training Program projects, one Innovative Practice Training Project of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, one research project supported by the Institute for Advanced Study, and one Shenzhen University Student Innovation and Development Fund project. During my undergraduate studies, I led a team that received a Finalist award (top 1% worldwide) in the Mathematical Contest in Modeling (MCM) and won over 20 honors and awards, including second place in the National University Physics Experiment Competition.
